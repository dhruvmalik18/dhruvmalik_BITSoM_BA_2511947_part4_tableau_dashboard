# Dashboard Story: Executive Sales Performance Report
**Prepared by:** Dhruv Malik | BITSoM_BA_2511947  
**Period:** January 2024 – December 2025  
**Audience:** Retail Leadership Team

---

## Executive Summary

The business generated **$217M in total sales** and **$33.3M in profit** (15.3% margin) over the two-year period. Revenue is growing year-over-year, the South region leads all geographies, and Technology is overwhelmingly the most profitable category. However, three risks demand leadership attention: heavy discounting eroding margins, Standard Class shipping creating delivery frustration, and a small cluster of Furniture sub-categories generating thin returns.

The dashboard tells a story in three acts: what is working well, where performance is lagging, and what leadership should decide today.

---

## What Is Performing Well

**Technology is carrying the business.** With 84% of total company profit coming from Technology — specifically Copiers, Phones, and Accessories — the product portfolio has a clear winner. These sub-categories should be the primary focus for inventory investment, marketing spend, and sales team incentives.

**The South region is the growth engine.** At $64.7M in sales and $9.99M in profit, the South outperforms the next-best region (North) by nearly $10M in revenue. Execution is clearly stronger there, and whatever is working in the South — channel strategy, team structure, or customer mix — should be studied and replicated.

**Revenue is trending up.** Year-over-year, 2025 sales exceed 2024 across most months. The company is growing, which validates current strategy at a broad level.

**Home Office segment is a strong contributor.** At $74.5M in segment sales, Home Office customers are the company's most valuable cohort today.

---

## What Is Underperforming

**Furniture and Office Supplies are margin laggards.** Together they account for less than 16% of total profit despite holding significant catalog space. Office Supplies' Paper, Binders, and Art sub-categories generate under $400K each in profit — a signal of either pricing weakness or a mismatch in promotional spend.

**The East and West regions are trailing.** At approximately $48.9M each, both regions lag South by over $15M. This gap is large enough to investigate whether it reflects market size differences, underinvestment, or execution gaps.

**Standard Class shipping is a service risk.** With an average delivery time of 4.7 days and majority order share, a significant portion of the customer base is receiving the slowest possible shipping experience. In a market where 2-day delivery is the expectation, this is a competitive vulnerability.

---

## What Risks Are Visible

**Discount abuse is destroying margin.** Orders with 30%+ discounts frequently produce negative profit per order. The correlation between discount level and profit is -0.27 — a meaningful negative relationship. Without a discount approval process, the sales team may be buying revenue at the cost of profitability.

**Furniture return costs are concentrated.** While the 4.5% overall return rate is not alarming, Furniture returns involve high unit values and high return shipping costs. This creates a disproportionate impact on net margin for that category.

**Revenue concentration risk.** Technology contributing 84% of profit means any supply disruption, pricing pressure from competitors, or demand shift in that category would have an outsized impact on company financial performance.

---

## What Opportunities Are Visible

**Referral and Organic channels outperform paid channels in conversion quality.** Campaign channel analysis suggests that not all channels produce equally profitable customers. Shifting budget toward higher-quality acquisition channels could improve margin per acquired customer.

**Corporate segment is underleveraged.** At $70.6M in sales, Corporate trails Home Office by $3.9M — a gap that could be closed with a targeted B2B outreach strategy. Corporate customers typically offer higher order values and lower churn risk.

**First Class and Same Day shipping present an upsell opportunity.** Customers using faster shipping modes also tend to have higher order values. Promoting shipping upgrades for high-value orders could increase revenue per order while improving customer satisfaction.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

1. **Introduce a discount authorization policy** — cap frontline discounts at 20%; require manager approval for anything beyond.
2. **Launch a South region playbook** — document and replicate the South's execution model in East and West.
3. **Invest in Technology inventory** — prioritize Copiers, Phones, and Accessories for Q3 stocking and seasonal promotion.
4. **Build a Corporate B2B growth program** — assign a dedicated sales team to corporate accounts with bundled pricing.
5. **Review Furniture and low-margin Office Supplies** — consider rationalization of sub-categories contributing less than $500K annually.
6. **Introduce shipping upgrade incentives** — offer First Class as a free upgrade for orders above $500.

---

## Limitations of the Dashboard

- The dataset covers two years (2024–2025); longer historical data would reveal stronger seasonality patterns.
- Customer-level data (CLV, retention, purchase frequency) is not available in this dataset, limiting lifetime value analysis.
- Geographic analysis is at the region level; state or city-level analysis could surface more specific opportunities.
- Campaign channel has some null values (~3% of records), limiting channel attribution confidence.
- Profit figures represent gross profit; no operating expense or overhead data is included.

---

## Suggested Next Analysis

- **Cohort analysis** by acquisition month to measure customer retention and repeat purchase rate.
- **RFM segmentation** (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) to identify the highest-value customer groups.
- **Discount elasticity study** — test whether removing high discounts reduces volume or merely margin.
- **Shipping satisfaction linkage** — correlate delivery days with customer rating to quantify the satisfaction cost of slow shipping.
- **State-level heat map** to identify geographic white spaces and over-indexed markets within regions.
